I don't know about you, but I recently got interested in the stock market due to the Gamestop and other meme stock hype. It's equally inspring and terrifying to see money grow and diminish at such astounding rates. If you timed it right and got lucky, you could have made enough money to retire in a single trade. Obviously, this opportunity is rather rare, but it shows the potential of making money in the market. This makes me wonder... what if machine learning could help us with trading?

## Theoretical Feasibility of Trading Stocks

I'm relatively new to the machine learning scene, with roughly one year of experience from college courses. However, by looking at recent advancements in machine learning and AI, it's not far-fetched to say that a machine can be taught to trade. AlphaGo beat world champions in a game that contains 10^360 possibilies. This is an AI that is pitted against the most genetically gifted players who have had decades of experience. Trading, by extension, should not be too complex to be mastered by machine learning. 

However, there are several difficulties that arise:
- Patterns and strategies that worked in the past, do not necessarliy work in the future
- Rules of trading change throughout time (SEC, etc.)
- Many factors which impact the market (Human psychology, foreign government policies, natural distasters, etc.)
- How many traders would be willing to compete against the algorithm in order to improve it?
- Insufficient data
- Etc.
